Unveiling Emotions: Text-based Emotion Detection

Project Overview

The project aimed to analyze the sentiment and emotions of Indonesian netizens regarding the government's ban on the "mudik" tradition during Ramadhan amidst the Covid-19 pandemic. The analysis utilized Emotion Recognition Analysis, a part of Natural Language Processing (NLP), to identify emotions expressed in tweets related to the topic.

Challenges

The challenge was to accurately classify emotions expressed in Indoensian tweets regarding the "mudik" ban during Ramadhan. Additionally, I needed to collect and preprocess a large dataset of from Twitter within a specified timeframe.

Process

1. Dictionary Initiation

Defined a set of predefined keywords related to human emotions, based on previous research by Philip R. Shaver and supplemented with additional manual definitions. After that, expanded by adding synonims using thesaurus for Bahasa Indonesia. At the end, all collected keywords need to be validated to ensure they accurately represented their assigned emotion class.

Preprocessing Input Data

Preprocessed Twitter data by tokenization, normalization, and keyword extraction.

Analysis of Similarity

Classified emotions using a cosine similarity algorithm to compare tweet keywords to the dictionary.

Result

The analysis showed that "Trust" and "Sadness" were the predominant emotions expressed in tweets related to the "mudik" ban. Trust appeared in approximately 24.6% of tweets, while Sadness appeared in around 21.7% of tweets. The analysis also revealed sentiments of anger, joy, surprise, fear, anticipation, and disgust among Indonesian netizens.

Data Stack

The project utilized a data stack consisting of Python, Twitter, NLP, Emotion Recognition
